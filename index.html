I was recently in the market for a new pair of jeans which prompted me to check if my name was still in vogue. It was bitter sweet to discover this domain name was available. As they say, beauty is fleeting.

I've been coding since 2008. Currently, I work remote full-time as a Ruby on Rails full-stack developer. Which means, I also write HTML, CSS, JavaScript. I actively follow news to stay up to date in each of the respective technologies I regularly use. I am a curious introvert and prefer to work with distributed teams but also willing to work on site for the right situation.

As a former Art Director, I still enjoy UI/Design tasks like wire framing, UX diagrams, and mentoring graphic designers who want to learn to code.

I also dribbble for fun.

Eat. Sleep. Code.

In 2007 I discovered Basecamp which lead me to Ruby on Rails (Rails). Rails inspired me to build my own project management tool which has taken longer than anticipated :-) I test with Minitest, monitor with New Relic, and deploy with Heroku. I am excited for Action Cable which will ship as part of Rails 5.

I prefer vanilla JS over CoffeeScript. The Rails apps I've worked with tend to use JQuery, Underscore, BackboneJS (and/or) AngularJS. I use Jasmine for testing. I am excited about the future of BabelJS, ECMA 6, and ReactJS.

I build static sites with Middleman because it is very similar to Rails. It's written in Ruby, has an Asset Pipeline, local server, and gems for developing with sophisticated tools. Then, with free static site hosting from GitHub that makes deployment easy and free!

I enjoy thoughtbot's front-end suite (Bourbon, Neat, and Bitters). I write SCSS and regularly try to apply methodologies like SMACSS and BEM as best I can.

HTML could be defined in broader terms as View management to include working with JS frameworks, Rails partials (ERB/SLIM), responsive web design, and cross browser testing.

B.C. (before coding)

During college I started a t-shirt business. The year was 2000 and the dot com bubble had just burst so I deduced the web was a fad and should focus on the apparel industry. It only took eight years to realize I was wrong.

I began to view code as a new form of literacy. Like many people, I had lots of Internet business ideas. But after several unsuccessful attempts with 3rd party sites, consultants, and other hacks I realized that I needed to learn to code for myself. So, I moved my family from Denver to Florida for three months so that I could attend starter league in Chicago. That was the catalyst I needed to make the switch.

In total, I spent 17 years in the apparel and music industries including - three turnarounds, founding two companies, and selling one. I have experience with acquiring profitable customers and I'm formally trained with a Masters in Finance.

Coding is my fifth career and by far my favorite of them all. My longest active commit streak is 193 days.
